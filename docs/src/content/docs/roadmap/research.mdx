<Aside type="caution" title="Research is not a promise">
Everything on this page is <strong>research-only</strong>. It informs
decisions; it does not become a claim until working, tested code exists and a
maintainer approves the support boundary. None of it is current support.
</Aside>

## External orchestration compatibility

<Status kind="research-only" /> CRI shims, Kubernetes node behavior, Docker
API shims, Testcontainers targets, full Compose parity, and
attach/exec/log-follow/port-forward compatibility were studied and **rejected
for current core**: those contracts require protocol, stream, lifecycle,
state-authority, identity, networking, logging, and scheduler behavior outside
Hostwright's local RuntimeAdapter scope. Any prototype requires a separate
maintainer-approved issue with an exact-version conformance target.

## Multi-host platform

<Status kind="research-only" /> Remote host agents, membership services, peer
discovery, state replication, remote mutation, remote placement, and scheduler
APIs were studied and **kept out of current core**: they require host
identity, enrollment, transport trust, state authority, quorum or replication
semantics, failure recovery, audit, and revocation models the local state
design deliberately does not have. Any future work would live in a separate
prototype or repository.

## Secure exposure

<Status kind="research-only" /> Tunnels (Cloudflare, Tailscale, WireGuard),
local DNS, reverse proxies, mTLS provisioning, and public exposure management
have research records and fail-closed policy blockers today. Hostwright-created
port publishes stay on `127.0.0.1`; anything broader is refused rather than
half-supported.

## Apple silicon accelerators

<Status kind="research-only" /> The accelerator investigation found **no
supported Apple-container path** for GPU, ANE, Metal, Core ML, MLX, or PyTorch
MPS acceleration inside managed Linux containers. Hostwright therefore ships
fail-closed accelerator policy decisions and makes no accelerator claims. Any
change requires an official supported access path, a local disposable proof, a
threat model, a policy gate, and maintainer approval.

## Advisory scheduling — the deliberate exception

<Status kind="implemented" /> Local **advisory** scheduling exists precisely
because it stops short of the research areas above: it produces deterministic
in-memory recommendations, scores, and reason codes from declared inputs and
existing policy decisions. It does not reserve capacity, place workloads,
expose a scheduler API, or schedule accelerators — and it stays advisory until
separate placement evidence exists.

## What would change these statuses

Every research area has the same reconsideration gate: a separate
maintainer-approved issue defining product placement (core, plugin, prototype,
or separate repository), an explicit behavior contract with non-goals, a state
and trust model, a disposable proof path, and documentation rules that keep
claims scoped to evidence. Until then, these remain research — documented so
you know they were considered, labeled so you never mistake them for features.
